# Calibration Session — Output T2-B

**Output:** T2-B (BBQ for 10, $80 task)
**Dimension in dispute:** Budget Compliance
**Rubric version at time of session:** v1 (pre-update)

---

## Step 1: Independent Scoring

Both evaluators scored all dimensions independently before comparing results.

**Evaluator 1 — Budget score: 0**
> "The output exceeds budget by $35–40, which is 44% over. The rubric says score 0 for exceeding budget with no acknowledgment. The note says 'trim some items' but doesn't specify what to actually remove in a way I'd call actionable."

**Evaluator 2 — Budget score: 1**
> "The output explicitly acknowledges it's over budget and names shrimp and cheaper cuts as specific things to remove. That's more than a vague comment — it gives the user a path forward. I read that as a 1."

| Dimension | Evaluator 1 | Evaluator 2 |

| Budget | **0** | **1** |
| Dietary | 2 | 2 |
| Realism | 2 | 2 |

Disagreement: Budget only.

---

## Step 3: Root Cause Analysis

The original rubric defined score 0 as:
> "Exceeds budget with no acknowledgment"

And score 1 as:
> "Exceeds budget by ≤20% OR minor violation with no explanation"

The rubric did not cover the case where the budget is exceeded by >20% AND the output acknowledges the violation AND proposes specific (but not fully detailed) corrective actions.

Both evaluators applied the rubric as written correctly. **The rubric was the problem, not the evaluators.**

---

## Step 4: Resolution

**Discussion outcome:** Evaluator 1 agreed that "skip the shrimp or get cheaper cuts" is more specific than "trim some items." Evaluator 2 agreed that the guidance stops short of fully actionable (no price impact stated, no alternative items suggested). Both agreed the output sits in a gray zone the rubric didn't define.

**Decision:** Score 1 is correct for T2-B. The threshold for a 1 is not the magnitude of the violation — it's the quality of the response to the violation.

---

## Step 5: Rubric Update (v1.1)

**Budget Compliance — Updated Level Definitions**

| Score | Definition |
|---|---|
| 2 | Meets budget exactly, or variance ≤5% with no note needed |
| 1 | Exceeds budget by any amount AND output explicitly acknowledges this AND proposes specific named corrective adjustments (e.g. "remove the shrimp" — not "trim some items") |
| 0 | Exceeds budget with no acknowledgment; OR exceeds budget with only vague guidance that is not actionable |

**Key clarification added:** Magnitude of violation does not determine the score. A 44% overage can still score 1 if the output handles it correctly.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Rubric gaps surface through calibration, not through rubric review.** This ambiguity was invisible until two evaluators hit it simultaneously.
2. **Disagreements between careful evaluators usually mean a rubric problem, not an evaluator problem.** Assume rubric ambiguity first.
3. **Score 0 should be reserved for clear failures.** When an output makes a genuine attempt to address a violation, a 0 misrepresents what the model actually did.
4. **Calibration examples become permanent anchors.** T2-B is now the canonical reference for what a Budget score of 1 looks like. Future evaluators compare against it directly.
