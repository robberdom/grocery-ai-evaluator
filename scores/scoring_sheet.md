## Dimension Notes

### T1-B
- **Budget (1):** Estimates "$50–60" — acknowledges potential overage but provides no specific items to remove. Scores 1, not 0 (acknowledgment exists), not 2 (no corrective specificity).
- **Realism (1):** 7-day meal list for a 3-day task. Misreads the scope constraint.
- **Completeness (1):** No per-person quantities, no prices on items.
- **Clarity (1):** No totals, no categories, no organization by day.

### T1-C
- **Budget (0):** $68 vs $50 — 36% over, zero acknowledgment.
- **Dietary (0):** Smoked salmon included. Not vegetarian. Mandatory 0.
- **Realism (0):** Truffle oil, heirloom tomatoes — specialty items presented as standard.
- **Completeness (1):** 3-day structure present but both core constraints violated.
- **Clarity (2):** Itemized list with prices. Ironically the best-structured output despite being the worst performer. Clarity and quality are independent — this is the rubric working correctly.

- **Note:** T3-B scores 6/10 total but is functionally disqualifying. Known limitation of summative scoring: a future rubric version should treat mandatory-0 dietary failures as automatic rejections regardless of total score.
